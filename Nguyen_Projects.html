App Inventor Project

Duration: September 18th - October 6th

This is my App Inventor Project, which was an assigned project in my Computer Science Principles class. The goal of this project was to modify a bland and simple game into a complex and fun game. I worked on this project with my partner Juan Jose. The game was called Oh My Spikes. This project needed a lot of creativity from Juan and I since you needed to change a game with no cool attributes into one that was actually entertaining and fun to play. Not to mention that Juan and I didn't have any knowledge whatsoever on how to use MIT App Inventor, so we had to make new features to the game while trying to figure out what each block of code did. Every day we would discuss and show each other how certain blocks of code work. We didn't continue making modifications until we fully understood the code. With my design creativity and exemplary communication with Juan, we were able to earn an A on the project. Our modifications for Oh My Spikes consisted of 3 new enemies for the bird, 2 that bounced around the screen and one that moved up and down, 4 different power-ups, coins scattered around the screen, and an instructions button that you could click on for the instructions to Oh My Spikes. This project taught me a lot. I learned how to make sprites bounce around the edge of the canvas, move up and down, and spawn randomly on the screen. I didn't just learn how to use MIT App Inventor. During the course of the project, I also learned how to have better communication by getting to know your partner better, and talking to your partner a lot about what you plan to do. All in all, this is my App Inventor Project.
